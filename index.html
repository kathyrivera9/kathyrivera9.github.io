About Me

I am Katherinne Rivera, a Data Systems Lead at Brighter Bites. During my time at Brighter Bites, my role has evolved from Data Analyst I to Data Analyst II, and now to my current role as Data Systems Lead. Throughout this progression, I have had hands-on experience in working in the front-end as well as in the backend of data. This includes everything from setting up data collection systems, to transforming collected data, then using data to create visual dashboards for reporting. This experience has revelead that I like working in the backend, so I seek to expand on the skills I have learned so far. Additionally, I understand that as we continue to develop, our technology advances with us. So I am always open and eager to learn something new. I believe stepping out of my comfort zone is a way to grow and learn. So I'm looking for my next challenge.

My Education

My education led me to the Texas A&M University where I earned my Bachelors degree in Applied Math with an emphasis in Computer Science and a minor in Computer Science. During my time in college, I discovered how much I enjoyed computer sciences and coding. My first programming class, an Intro to Java, showed me how a couple lines of code can create ideas into reality, which opened my eyes to the endless possibilities. My Applied Mathematics background, sets me apart since I learned to approach programming not only from a technical perspective, but also through the lens of algorithm and analytical reasoning. I am confident in the programming foundation I built in college to propel my to learning to more programming languages and expand my technical skills.

My learning did not stop at college. After graduating, I have taken it upon myself to continue expanding my technical skills, learning new languages such as SQL, and and strengthening my understanding of data modeling and database design. On my own, I've take advantage of the internet resources to learn SQL fundamentals and Data Visualizations through Tableau. At Brighter Bites, I've put these skills to practice where they continued to grow. Additionally, I learned how to use Power BI, data modeling and database design as a Data Analyst. Over time, I built interactive dashboards and visual reports for staff and upper management. These projects taught me the value of collaboration and how to translate data into insights that drive real decisions. In my current role as Data Systems Lead, I have become more involved in maintaining, optimizing, and creating our database ETL pipelines ensuring our data systems are efficient, scalable, and reliable.
